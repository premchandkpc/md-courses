🌀 PACELC Architecture

PACELC theorem extends CAP: in normal operation (no partition), there is a trade-off between latency and consistency (L vs C).

Flow: Partitioned (P) → Choose Availability (A) vs Consistency (C). Else (E, no partition) → Choose Latency (L) vs Consistency (C).

🌀 PA/EL Systems

DynamoDB, Cassandra, DNS: Prefer availability and low latency. Accept eventual consistency. Best for user-facing apps where speed matters.

🏛️ PC/EC Systems

Spanner, ZooKeeper, etcd: Prefer consistency over latency. Use for coordination, metadata, financial transactions.

📊 PA/EC Systems

PostgreSQL with synchronous replication: choose availability during partitions but consistency in normal operation.

⚡ Real-World

No system is purely one model. Most use tunable consistency (Cassandra NWR, DynamoDB global tables).
