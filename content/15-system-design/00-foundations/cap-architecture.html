🏛️ CAP Theorem Architecture

The fundamental distributed systems trade-off: Consistency, Availability, Partition Tolerance — choose at most two. In practice, P is mandatory.

Flow: Network Partition occurs → must choose: wait for consistency (CP) or serve with stale data (AP). All practical systems pick AP or CP.

🏛️ CP Systems

ZooKeeper, etcd, Consul: Stop accepting writes during partition until consistency restored.

🟢 AP Systems

DynamoDB, Cassandra, S3: Accept writes on both sides of partition, reconcile later.

⚠️ Partition Tolerance

P is not optional in distributed systems — networks will partition. Real choice is always CP vs AP.

💡 Practical Reality

Most databases let you tune this per-request (Cassandra: QUORUM vs ONE, DynamoDB: consistent reads).

C — ConsistencyEvery read returns the most recent write or error.
A — AvailabilityEvery request gets a (non-error) response, even if stale.
P — Partition ToleranceSystem continues operating despite network partitions.
RealityP is always chosen. The real trade-off is CP vs AP.
