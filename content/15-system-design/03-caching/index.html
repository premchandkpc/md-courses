Choosing the Right Policy

LRU is the default choice for most workloads — works well when recency correlates with probability of access. LFU beats LRU for content with stable popularity distributions (e.g., CDN caching). FIFO is never the right choice except for caches with uniform TTL. TTL-only is essential for time-sensitive data (auth tokens, rate limit counters). Most production caches combine LRU + TTL: evict by LRU among expired entries first.
